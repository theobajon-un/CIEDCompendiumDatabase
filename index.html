Background

Improvised explosive devices (IEDs) pose a significant, multi-faceted and evolving threat to peace, security, stability, and development worldwide. The UN General Assembly and Security Council have repeatedly recognised this threat and called on Member States to develop and share effective national measures.

In Resolution A/RES/79/53 (2024), the UNGA reiterated its call on States to share information with UNIDIR — including lessons learned and national experiences — to strengthen the development of an online compendium of effective national measures.

This Compendium is UNIDIR's response to that mandate. It provides a structured, anonymised and searchable repository of practical measures drawn from national experiences across all regions, intended to inform and inspire States and other relevant stakeholders in their counter-IED efforts.

About this Compendium

Building a Shared Knowledge Base on Counter-IED Measures

Between 2020 and 2025, at least 18 States from different regions voluntarily used UNIDIR's Counter-IED Capability Maturity Model and Self-Assessment Tool (CMM/SAT). Yet despite this global engagement, States have lacked a shared resource to exchange information and practical measures to counter threats posed by IEDs.

In Resolution A/RES/79/53 (2024), the UN General Assembly reiterated its call on interested and affected States to use UNIDIR's model and tool, and encouraged all States to share — on a voluntary basis — information with UNIDIR, "including details of good practices, lessons learned and national experiences in adopting measures to prevent and counter [IEDs], in order to strengthen the development of an online compendium of effective national measures."

This platform is UNIDIR's direct response to that mandate. Developed by the Conventional Weapons Programme, it consolidates practical, anonymised measures drawn from national experiences across all regions, structured around the UN Whole-of-System Approach to Countering IEDs.

Methodological Framework

A Rigorous, Multi-Source Approach

The Compendium is grounded in UNIDIR's Counter-IED Capability Maturity Model and Self-Assessment Tool (CMM/SAT) and structured around the UN Whole-of-System (WoS) Approach to Countering IEDs, as reflected in the 2024 Report of the UN Secretary-General on measures to counter improvised explosive devices. Information was collected, analysed and consolidated from three complementary sources, ensuring both depth and breadth of coverage across regions and capability areas.

CMM/SAT Self-Assessments

Between 2020 and 2025, at least 18 Member States across different regions voluntarily applied UNIDIR's CMM/SAT. The structured outputs of these self-assessments form a core evidence base, offering detailed, standardised insights into national C-IED capabilities and measures.

Structured Questionnaires

In early 2026, UNIDIR circulated a structured questionnaire to all UN Member States, inviting voluntary contributions of lessons learned and national experiences across the components of the UN WoS Approach. All responses are treated with full confidentiality.

Open-Source Research

Complementary background research — drawing on official government sources, UN documents, academic literature, and open data — enriches the evidence base, providing additional context and helping ensure comprehensive coverage across all regions and components.

From Data to Compendium: The Process

1
Collection

Information was gathered through the three complementary sources described above, covering all 19 components of the UN Whole-of-System Approach and all regions. Participation by Member States and partners was entirely voluntary.

2
Consolidation & Analysis

Collected information was systematically reviewed, coded and consolidated by component. Measures addressing similar challenges or following comparable approaches were grouped to surface cross-national patterns and draw out practical insights of broad relevance.

3
Anonymisation

All entries in this Compendium are anonymised as a matter of principle, ensuring that no specific State is identified. This approach reflects the preferences of some contributors, but has been applied universally to ensure consistency and equitable treatment of all contributors.

4
Expert Review & Validation

The consolidated entries were individually reviewed and validated by an independent expert reference group, comprising national and international experts in C-IED, security policy, and related fields. This review process ensures rigour, accuracy, and relevance across all components.

Analytical Framework: The UN Whole-of-System Approach

The UN WoS Approach organises C-IED activities into 19 components — covering both upstream (preventive) and downstream (responsive) activities, as well as cross-cutting issues — structured around the IED event timeline. This framework underpins the structure of the Compendium's database.

UNIDIR CMM/SAT — Five Levels of Maturity

Source: UNIDIR C-IED CMM/SAT

Level 1
Initial — Ad hoc activities, no formalised approach
Level 2
Repeatable — Some recurring practices, limited documentation
Level 3
Defined — Standardised procedures established and documented
Level 4
Managed — Quantitative monitoring and performance management
Level 5
Optimising — Continuous improvement and innovation embedded

Note: The UNIDIR CMM/SAT was developed around a 16-component model. The Compendium is additionally structured to align with the 19-component UN Whole-of-System Approach as referenced in the 2024 Report of the Secretary-General on measures to counter improvised explosive devices.

About the CMM/SAT

The UNIDIR Counter-IED Capability Maturity Model and Self-Assessment Tool (CMM/SAT) provides a structured methodology for States to assess their national C-IED capabilities on a five-level maturity scale, enabling States to identify gaps, track progress, and prioritise areas for improvement.
